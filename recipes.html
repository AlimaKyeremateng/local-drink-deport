Traditional Ghanaian Drink Recipes

Learn to prepare authentic beverages just like our ancestors did

Sobolo (Hibiscus Tea)

45 mins 6 servings

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried hibiscus petals
  • 1 medium pineapple (peeled and chopped)
  • 1 piece ginger (thumb-sized, peeled)
  • 1 cup sugar or honey
  • 10 cloves
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • Water
  • Ice cubes

Preparation:

  1. Rinse hibiscus petals thoroughly in cold water
  2. In a large pot, combine hibiscus, pineapple, ginger, cloves and cinnamon with 8 cups water
  3. Bring to boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes
  4. Strain mixture through fine mesh sieve into a pitcher
  5. Stir in sugar or honey while still warm
  6. Chill completely and serve over ice
Grandma's Tip: Add a splash of pineapple juice before serving for extra sweetness!

Lamogin (Millet Drink)

2 days 8 servings

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups millet flour
  • 1 cup corn dough
  • 1 teaspoon ginger powder
  • ½ cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 ripe banana (optional)
  • Water

Preparation:

  1. Mix millet flour with 4 cups warm water in a large bowl
  2. Add corn dough and mix thoroughly
  3. Cover with clean cloth and let ferment for 24-48 hours
  4. Strain through fine sieve into a clean container
  5. Add ginger powder and mashed banana (if using)
  6. Dilute with water to desired consistency
  7. Add sugar to taste and chill before serving
Pro Tip: The longer it ferments, the tangier it becomes - taste daily until perfect!

Asaana (Fermented Corn)

3 days 10 servings

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups white corn
  • 1 cup millet (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Sugar to taste
  • Water

Preparation:

  1. Soak corn and millet in water for 2 days, changing water twice daily
  2. On third day, drain and grind into smooth paste
  3. Mix with 8 cups water and strain through muslin cloth
  4. Allow liquid to settle for 4 hours
  5. Carefully pour off top water, keeping the sediment
  6. Mix sediment with fresh water (ratio 1:3)
  7. Add salt and sugar, then boil while stirring for 15 minutes
  8. Cool and serve chilled
Traditional Note: Some families add a bit of coconut milk for extra richness!

Burkina (Spiced Sorghum)

1 day 6 servings

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sorghum flour
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cloves
  • ½ teaspoon grains of selim (hwentia)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • Water

Preparation:

  1. Dry roast sorghum flour in pan until aromatic (5-7 mins)
  2. In a pot, boil 6 cups water with spices for 15 minutes
  3. Strain out spices and return flavored water to pot
  4. Gradually whisk in sorghum flour to avoid lumps
  5. Simmer on low heat for 20 minutes, stirring constantly
  6. Add sugar and stir until dissolved
  7. Cool to room temperature then refrigerate
  8. Serve chilled with ice cubes
Variation: Add a splash of lime juice before serving for a citrusy twist!

Samia (Tigernut Drink)

8 hours 4 servings

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups tigernuts (soaked overnight)
  • 1 cup coconut flesh
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
  • 3-4 dates (pitted)
  • Water
  • Ice cubes

Preparation:

  1. Drain soaked tigernuts and rinse thoroughly
  2. Blend tigernuts with coconut and 4 cups water until smooth
  3. Strain through cheesecloth into a bowl, squeezing out all liquid
  4. Return milk to blender with dates, vanilla and nutmeg
  5. Blend until dates are completely incorporated
  6. Strain again for smoother texture
  7. Chill for at least 4 hours
  8. Serve cold with ice cubes
Health Note: Rich in vitamin E and great for digestion - a true superfood drink!
